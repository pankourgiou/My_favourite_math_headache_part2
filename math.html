Electrostatics
Coulomb's Law
Two charges either attract or repel. The force drops with the square of the distance — double the separation, quarter the force. The same law governs gravity, light, and your hair on a dry day. // One proton and one electron at 1 Å apart: 230 nN. Tiny but mighty. You are held together by this.
F = k · q₁q₂ / r² k = 8.9875×10⁹ N·m²/C² Units: Coulombs, metres, Newtons
Opposite charges attract. Same charges repel. Obviously.

Number Theory
Stirling's Approximation
Computing 1000! is painful. Stirling says: don't. Use ln(n!) ≈ n·ln(n) − n instead. The approximation gets relatively better as n grows. At n=1000 it's off by 0.008%. Not bad for a lazy formula. // The full version adds ½ln(2πn). Even lazier people use just n·ln(n)−n and pray.
ln(n!) ≈ n·ln(n) − n + ½·ln(2πn) Stirling's formula: n! ≈ √(2πn)·(n/e)ⁿ Error → 0 as n → ∞
